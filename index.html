A Bit About Me

As a new resident to the Denver area, I dove head first into the worldof programming, but before that I have had a very interesting journey. After graduating with a Bachelor's of Science in Exercise Science from Northern Arizona University, that journey officially began.

Upon receiving my diploma I was the closest thing to a deer in the headlights besides an actual deer in the headlights. I was working part-time as a Barista when I decided to pursure a career in medicine. For two years after I was working as an Emergency Medical Technician in Orange County, California and Seattle, Washington. After countless hours of volunteering at medical centers, days on end of shadowing physicians, and the most stressful test in the world (I'm talking about you, MCAT), I knew that this path wasn't for me. If your heart isn't into being a doctor you really shouldn't be one.

I battled with justifying my decision to abandon the road I had been on the past few years, but ultimately chose to take everything little thing I had learned and channel it in a different direction. That's when I stepped into the world of Tech. I had the good fortune of being hired at Zillow Group, a company that I consider more of a home than an employer.

At Zillow, I worked in a technical support-styled role, and learned an immense amount in my time there. Eight months in and I was being put on projects that opened the doors to a realm, and one that was completely foreign to me: Programming. I never thought it'd be acheivable for me to become a developer since I didn't get a degree in Computer Science or any of those related fields. So I put that thought on the backburner.

Afte a year at Zillow, my lovely wife (who has been supporting my convulated career path since college) and I made a life altering decision to quit our jobs and travel abroad for six months. Might I add she was very successful in her role at Zillow (yes, we worked at the same company), and still wanted to take on this outlandish adventure. So we quit, packed up our apartment, and put our life in a storage unit. Good-Bye United States!

The next six months we traveled through Southeast Asia and South America, three months in each. From trekking through the Himalayas in Nepal, to volunteering with rescued elephants and dogs in Thailand, to traversing Patagonia, to eating our hearts out in every place we went.
